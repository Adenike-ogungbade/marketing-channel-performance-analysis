# Marketing Channel Performance Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes the performance of different marketing acquisition channels to determine which channel generates the highest Return on Investment (ROI), strongest profit contribution, and best long-term customer retention.

The goal of the analysis is to help marketing leadership identify the most efficient channels for scaling customer acquisition.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains three main tables:

### campaign_performance
Tracks marketing campaign metrics including:
- marketing spend
- impressions
- clicks
- total users acquired
- activated users

### revenue_analysis
Tracks revenue generated by users from each channel, including:
- active users
- ARPU (Average Revenue per User)
- total revenue

### user_cohorts
Tracks long-term retention of users acquired from each marketing channel.

---

## Tools Used
- SQL  
- Marketing Analytics  
- Cohort Analysis  

---

## Key Metrics Analyzed

### Return on Investment (ROI)
Revenue generated relative to marketing spend.

### Profit
Total revenue generated minus marketing spend.

### Customer Retention
Percentage of users who remain active over time.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Referral marketing is the most profitable channel
Referral campaigns generated the highest ROI (11.34) and the highest total profit.

### 2. Referral users have the strongest long-term retention
Approximately **74% of referral users remain active after 18 months**, indicating very strong customer loyalty.

### 3. Google Ads delivers moderate performance
Google Ads produces solid revenue and ROI but shows steady decline in retention over time.

### 4. Paid Social drives lower-quality users
Retention drops significantly for Paid Social users, with only **6% remaining active after 18 months**.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Increase investment in referral marketing programs.
- Continue optimizing Google Ads campaigns targeting high-intent users.
- Improve audience targeting and campaign strategy for Paid Social marketing.
